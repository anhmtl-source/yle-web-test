Swans

Swans are the largest birds in the duck family. There are six different kinds swans which have white bodies, but in a few parts of the world there are black swans too.

Swans are clever and remember people who have kind or unkind to them! A frightened really angry swan can break a man’s leg its strong wings!

Swans live on rivers and lakes. But in the winter months, some might live near the sea because salt water is usually a little than river water.

Swans don’t usually fly very far from the place they build their nests.

It takes between 35 and 42 days for a baby swan, which called a ‘cygnet’, to break open its egg!

A baby swan can be grey white.

A swan eats underwater plants. It finds these by its long neck deep into the water and its tail up into the air!

Swans might eat small frogs and insects.
